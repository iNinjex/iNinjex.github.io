Sears Website Redesign Case Study

This case study documents the redesign process for a Sears-inspired retail website. The goal was to improve the shopping experience by creating clearer navigation, better page organization, and a more modern visual layout.

Problem

The original experience felt difficult to navigate because users needed a clearer way to browse categories, search for products, access help pages, and move through the shopping process. A retail website needs to help users find products quickly, understand where they are, and complete actions like signing in, adding items to a cart, or finding support.

My Role

This was a team project, but my focus was on improving the front-end structure, page layout, navigation experience, and usability of the website. I worked on making the site easier to move through across pages such as Home, Search, Product, Cart, About, Help, Sign In, and Sign Up.

Process

I began by reviewing the existing page structure and identifying areas where the user flow could be improved. The main focus areas were navigation, spacing, visual hierarchy, page consistency, and responsive layout. From there, I adjusted the design so the website felt more organized and easier to use.

Design Decisions

Several decisions were made to improve usability. The navigation was made more consistent across pages so users could move between major sections without confusion. Product and category areas were organized visually so users could quickly understand what each page was for. Buttons, links, and page sections were styled consistently to make the interface feel more complete and professional.

Testing and Feedback

The redesign was tested by opening the site in the browser, clicking through the navigation, checking page links, and reviewing the layout across different screen sizes. Feedback and testing helped reveal where spacing, page linking, and mobile navigation needed improvement. Based on that testing, I adjusted links, improved layout consistency, and made sure the site worked as a complete multi-page experience.

Final Product

The final website is a multi-page retail redesign with pages for products, search, cart, deals, help, account access, and category browsing. Compared to the original concept, the final version provides a cleaner structure, clearer navigation, and a more complete shopping experience.
